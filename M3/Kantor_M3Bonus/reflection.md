1. What surprised you about working with AI?

I used Gemini to complete this project. It's amazing how fast any of these LLM's can gather information and regurgitate it back to you. My issue, however, with Gemini and ChatGPT, is how much fluff it gives you. I don't need it complimenting me on how good of an idea it is, or "Good job for thinking that way". I am asking it for an answer not compliments.


2. What was harder than expected? (AI can’t read your mind!)

What was harder than I expected is how smart dumb AI is. What I mean by that is it is extremely intelligent in the sense that it can literally tell you anything, just not good enough. I have to constantly tell it the same thing multiple times in different forms just for it to finally grasp what I want it to do.


3. What was easier than expected?

What was easier than I expected is I only had to type a few sentences in order for the LLM to produce finished code. It was saved me time and effort. If I were to produce the exact code it gave me, it easily would've taken me 2-3 hours as I am not proficient in C++, switches and various methods included within the code I received.


4. Would you use AI differently now? How?

I would switch over to Calude. It seems to be the best LLM on the market right now. It seems to lead in coding specific prompts and I know many people who use it and swear by it. I have used ChatGPT, Gemini and CoPilot, but they give me more fluff than answers. I will have to pass parameters to the LLM prior to giving it a request.


5. What concepts clicked for you during this project?

I do not think any concepts "clicked" for me. Many of which was presented to me was basic understanding of functions and the C++ language we had already learned in CSC-134. All it was, was implementing it in a certain flow to work within various functions. The game at the end of the day is very simple and can be built by somebody within 2 weeks of learning to code.
